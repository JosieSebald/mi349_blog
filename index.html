Hello folks, welcome to my blog page! Here, I will talk about my top three favorite games, music genres, and art pieces.

Favorite Games

  1. Minecraft
  2. Left 4 Dead 2
  3. Stardew Valley

The first game in my list is Minecraft, a cube-based sandbox game released in 2011- and I've been playing it since. Minecraft is the first major game that I played growing up, and it began my very invested hobby of playing video games that are singeplayer and multiplayer.
The second game in my list is Left 4 Dead 2, a survival horror first-person shooter that can be played by yourself or with friends. I choose to play this game with my friends, and I like it because it gives me an adrenaline rush and provides many different challenges and game modes- not to mention, killing zombies with a baseball bat is a great way to release anger.
The third and final game in my list is Stardew Valley, which is a simulation RPG. It is also a great game to play with friends; you can go mining, fishing, complete quests, participate in festivals, and many other things. It's a very friendly game and fun to look at.

Favorite Music Genres

  • Pop
  • Rock
  • Indie

Pop is a favorite of mine because it is very upbeat and fun to dance to. It makes me feel very colorful and happy.
Rock is another great genre because I love the sounds of guitars and drums; I think they work very well together, and they give off a sense of power and independence.
Indie is the other genre in this list and I love it because I get to hear voices from so many people that aren't typically mainstream. These independent artists have a lot to contribute and I think they should be showcased more often.

Art Pieces

  • Blender Rendered Classsroom, 2019
  • Cat Commission, 2021
  • College Art Assignment, 2020

One of the images I'm most proud of is my Blender Classroom Render. I made this my senior year of high school for a VR game. I spent many months on it and it turned out great. It was a super fun project to work on and I hope that, in the future, I get to do something similar again!
Another image that I loved working on is my College Art Assignment. In this piece, we had to draw objects we were looking at, and they had to be objects from nature. Just the pineapple took about six hours, with the whole drawing taking about nine hours in total. Though it was a pain to draw, it was really fun and paid off in the end.
Another great piece that I've produced is a Cat Commission, which is my most recent piece of work. This was commissioned to me by a friend, and the picture is an image of his cat sleeping in a group of pink clouds. This took some hours of work but I always enjoy working on digital art, so each moment was a good one.
